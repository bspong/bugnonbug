On Linux, click on select tag fails to recognize hover state
User-Agent: Mozilla/5.0 (X11; U; Linux i686; en-US; rv:1.8.0.1) Gecko/20060124 Firefox/1.5.0.1
Build Identifier: Mozilla/5.0 (X11; U; Linux i686; en-US; rv:1.8.0.1) Gecko/20060124 Firefox/1.5.0.1

Suppose there is a div:hover-style defined and then there is a <div> using this style and it ha a form with a <select> tag. On Linux (not on Windows), with clicking and opening the pull-down menu the hover-state isn't recognized anymore until you move the mouse. (So in order to reproduce, don't move the mouse after clicking).

Subsequent mouse movement will turn hover state on again but it result in flickering.


Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
HTML-Code:
<html>
<head>
<style type="text/css">
<!--
div {width:200px;background-color:lightyellow;text-align:center;height:100px;}
div:hover {background-color:green;}

-->
</style>
</head>

<form>
<div>
<select>
<option>Option1</option>
<option>Option2</option>
</select>
</div>
</form>

</body>
</html>

Actual Results:
<div> flicker to yellow background upon click on the select-box.

Expected Results:
<div> should stay green even through opening of the pull-down menu.

OS: Linux Fedora Core 2, Matrox X-Server module
