"What are Live Bookmarks?" link in Subscription Options preference panel open "phantom" new window
User-Agent: Mozilla/5.0 (Windows; U; Windows NT 5.0; en-US; rv:1.8.1b1) Gecko/20060708 BonEcho/2.0b1
Build Identifier: Mozilla/5.0 (Windows; U; Windows NT 5.0; en-US; rv:1.8.1b1) Gecko/20060708 BonEcho/2.0b1

When you click on the "What are Live Bookmarks?" link in the Subscription Options preference panel, a new Firefox Window opens. This window load the Live Bookmarks explanation page at Mozilla.com, but the page title in the taskbar show the page title of the tab that wa open when the option menu wa opened, not the Live Bookmarks page's. Closing the Window return you to the window in which you had the Preferences dialog open.

Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1. Open Firefox to any page or pages.
2. Open the Options dialog.
3. Click "Choose Feed Reader" from the General page.
4. Click the "What are Live Bookmarks?" link.

Actual Results:
A new window opens, but doe not create a new taskbar entry, and show the previously open page's title.

Expected Results:
A new window or tab open when the link is clicked.
