Recently, Google rolled out a new feature to enable blogger to chat with their site visitor through the web (gtalk is not required).

You can login with you gmail account then get the Ifame for here: http://www.google.com/talk/service/badge/New

Sense, I'm using Linux, I have to use the web-embedded chat in my Gmail account. This is where Firefox crash. It crashed when I wa testing that feature by chatting with my self. All you have to do is click Pop-out on the chat. Follow step exactly!


Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1.Login with you gmail account
2.go to http://www.google.com/talk/service/badge/New
3.Copy the Ifram html source and save it to a file on you desktop (test.html)
4.Go to you gmail account an sign in to your embedded chat
5-Open test.html (Your online status should be green) and click on your account name. This should open a new window for the chat.
6.Go back in your gmail inbox, a little html chat window should be in to bottom right corner or the browser. Click Pop-out.
Actual Results:
Firefox try to open a new window for the chat then it the browser hang for ever with 100% CPU utilization.

Expected Results:
The same chat window that wa opened from test.html, should contain open a new "HTML" tab for the other chat (the one opened from gmail inbox by clicking pop-out)
