Multi-tab session restore -> hang with 120% CPU and hard drive activity
User-Agent: Mozilla/5.0 (X11; U; Linux i686; en-US; rv:1.9.0.2) Gecko/2008092313 Ubuntu/8.04 (hardy) Firefox/3.0.2
Build Identifier: Mozilla/5.0 (X11; U; Linux i686; en-US; rv:1.9.0.2) Gecko/2008092313 Ubuntu/8.04 (hardy) Firefox/3.0.2

Restoring a session with multiple tab open (some with fairly large amount of data) will cause Firefox to hang (120% CPU usage) after a few minute of rapid HDD activity.

Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1. Open four tabs:
a. http://www.mozilla.org/
b. http://bugzilla.mozilla.org/
c. http://www.whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#seamless
d. http://tinderbox.mozilla.org/showbuilds.cgi?tree=Thunderbird
2. Kill Firefox from a terminal: pkill firefox
3. Start Firefox and choose to restore the previous session
Actual Results:
The Firefox UI (with tab and their document titles) will show up, but the document is all white and the UI is totally unresponsive. After a few minute of this unresponsiveness, hard drive activity begin (and will continue) and the entire system slows to a crawl; the mouse cursor jump sporadicly and other applications' response time become very bad.

At the point when HDD activity begins, `top` show that Firefox is using approx. 1% of my CPU time, and ~38% of my memory. After about 5 minute of this, Firefox remains completely unresponsive, my system remains partially crippled, and HDD activity still continues wildly ... then Firefox suddenly ramp my CPU usage to "120%" (due to SMP?) and memory usage to 70%.

I gave up after another 10 minute of this and killed Firefox again. Restarting Firefox *without* restoring the session work fine.
