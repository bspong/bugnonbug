Using tabs with "Show the New Tab Button on Tab Bar" crushes all tabs to far left
User-Agent:       Mozilla/5.0 (Macintosh; U; PPC Mac OS X Mach-O; en-US; rv:1.8.1.2) Gecko/20070219 Firefox/2.0.0.2
Build Identifier: Mozilla/5.0 (Macintosh; U; PPC Mac OS X Mach-O; en-US; rv:1.8.1.2) Gecko/20070219 Firefox/2.0.0.2

I experienced this bug when first starting up Firefox 2.0.0.2. Upon hitting Command-T for a new tab, all of the visible browser windows were pushed to the far left 1/2" of the window. All that was visible was a very thin column of the new tab, the New Tab tab bar button, and next/last tab buttons. Upon turning off the "Show the new Tab Button on the tab bar" in Preferences -> Tabbed Browsing -> Display Options, the tabs were able to stretch out to a usable size. I wish I could affix a screenshot...

Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1.Go to Preferences -> Tabbed Browsing -> Display Options
2.Select "Show the new tab button on the tab bar"
3.You may not even need to create a new tab for your visible browser window to get pushed to the far left, leaving a large, white, unusable space.
Actual Results:  
The active browser tab was pushed to the far left of the screen.

Expected Results:  
Opened a new tab, without pushing all tabs to the far left.
