I've had, lately, a series of crashes in Fox, as wells as in Fox-SafeMode.

Much of the time, there is no rhyme or reason.  Sometimes I have the sense that it is connected to Javascript, or video or other imagery that shows on NYTIMES.COM or CNN.COM [connected with flash?], and sometimes I suspect that scrolling my middle mouse button will do it.  Sometimes I wonder, for no particular reason, if it is the Sprint wireless networking software.  Other times, I suspect changing tabs or scrolling -while- a page is loading; other times, I am doing nothing and that particular suspicion is misplaced.

I've been tempted to suspect windows, or hardware faults, but this only occurs with Fox.   I've tried, with an "art" approach to solving the problem rather than an engineer's knowledge, to avoid crashes by using SQLite to compact Fox's databases; sometimes I have the sense that that works, but sometimes, it doesn't.   Generally, I have multiple tabs open on crashes, but this is the consequence of my usage; I've had crashes with just one tab open, or my local, static, homepage filled with a short list of usable URL's.  Sometimes, I notice that the crash occurs 5-10 seconds after I restart Fox, with or without restoring tabs.   Usually, restarting windows might "fix" things.   Although sometimes, the crashes just stop [which makes me think a database issue, for some reason].    Note....This crash behavior is limited to Fox [though once in a rare while Thunderbird catches the same bug-iness].   Sometimes the crashes stop, until a new Fox update appears, and then I fall into a pattern, again -- 3.08 and .07 both seemed to have the problem more than the next earlier version [though I would not swear this..]
