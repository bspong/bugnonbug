Links in PDF-Documents are handled not as expected
User-Agent:       Mozilla/5.0 (Windows; U; Windows NT 5.1; de; rv:1.8.0.1) Gecko/20060111 Firefox/1.5.0.1
Build Identifier: Mozilla/5.0 (Windows; U; Windows NT 5.1; de; rv:1.8.0.1) Gecko/20060111 Firefox/1.5.0.1

When in Firefox a PDF-document is displayed that contains links to the same or different websites and you want to navigate those links you get the following message from Adobe Reader after a while: The web browser has not responded to your request for over 1 minute: terminating request. At the same time the link was followed and displayd correctly in Firefox. If you then click the button of the Adobe Reader message, the link is followed a second time and often not displayed as expected. Pages with frames are displayed in the body part of the former page.

Reproducible: Always

Steps to Reproduce:
1.first go to the above mentioned website
2.then navigate to the page Turnierplan
3.there you will find a PDF-Document with a lot a hypertext-links
4.try one of the links
5.you have arrived the problem
Actual Results:  
After a while you get the error message of the adobe reader and after answering that message the page is diplayed a second time, often with interlocking frames. 

Expected Results:  
Displaying the referenced page immediately

Version of Adobe reader is 7.07
Problem also on Windows 2000
Problem not with the Browsers Internet Explorer, Netscape Browser, Mozilla, Opera and T-Online Browser.
